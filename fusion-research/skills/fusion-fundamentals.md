# Nuclear Fusion Fundamentals

## Key Reactions
- D-T (deuterium-tritium): Highest cross-section at achievable temperatures, produces 17.6 MeV
- D-D (deuterium-deuterium): No tritium breeding needed, lower yield
- D-He3: Aneutronic, but requires higher temperatures (~100 keV)

## Critical Parameters
- Lawson criterion: n·τ·T > threshold for ignition
- Beta (β): Ratio of plasma pressure to magnetic pressure
- Q factor: Fusion power output / heating power input
- Triple product: n·T·τE (density × temperature × confinement time)

## Confinement Approaches
- Magnetic confinement (tokamaks, stellarators, spheromaks)
- Inertial confinement (laser-driven, Z-pinch)
- Magnetic mirror, field-reversed configurations

When calculating fusion parameters, use SI units. Reference ITER parameters as a benchmark for tokamak designs.
